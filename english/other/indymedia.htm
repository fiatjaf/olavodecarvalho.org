The site which with exemplary cynicism calls itself “Centro de Mídia Independente” (Center of Independent Media) lost its last scruples and progressed to criminality pure and simple.

CMI is a millionaire NGO, with offices in more than a hundred countries, hell bent on making anti-American and anti-Israeli propaganda, exactly on the same line of thousands of nazi and communist sites. Yet they have no shame in calling “fascist” those who take stands opposite to theirs.

Desperate at not being able to answer the denuciations I present in my articles and in Mídia Sem Máscara1, above all about the alliance of the so-called “pacifists” with neo-nazi movements, the site is now calling for the method of “direct action”, the same used by Mussolini to cut “evil” at its roots, by eliminating me physically.

I do not feel any inclination whatsoever to trust my safety to the leftist authorities that govern us, which have already proved to be more interested in protecting the Farcs from the US than in protecting the Brazilian people from the Farcs and other criminal organizations associated with their party in the S�o Paulo Forum, the strategic coordination of the communist movement on the continent.

I do not have therefore, the most minimal guarantees for my life or work. I am outside of constitutional rights and excluded from that “citizenship”.

To me, it is of no importance what happens to me. Dying is not a disease or reason for shame. Shame is living in a country where only murderers have the protection of the authorities. Shame is to be member of a professional class in which the preaching of homicide bears the label of “fight against intolerance”.

I am ashamed of belonging to a people who elect as president a friend of the Farc, I am ashamed of having as professional colleagues the scoundrels from CMI.

MÍDIA SEM MÁSCARA, APRIL 1st, 2003

From the editorial staff of MSM – The website CMI (Centro de Mídia Independente /\ Independent Media Center), diffusor of death threats to the philosopher and journalist Olavo de Carvalho, is the Brazilian subsidiary of the American Indymedia. CMI was started in 2000 by an initiative of anti-globalisation activists in São Paulo. CMI has its headquarters in the building of the Institute of Culture and Liberal Action (ICAL): Pça. Américo Jacomino, 89 – Sumaré – São Paulo – SP (next to the Vila Madalena subway station) – Phone: +55 (11) 3865-7028. Open from Monday to Friday from 2:00 PM to 7:00 PM. There is a cybercafe there.

One of the main persons responsible for CMI, working on it almost full time, is Pablo Ortellado, 30 years old, M.A. in History at USP4 and studying for a PhD also in USP (imagine, he has been granted a scholarship by the university to spend practically all day taking care of CMI).

Ortellado had his leg broken during a march in the Paulista Avenue against FTAA, in April, 2001, which resulted in confusion and was repressed by the Military Police. He is also responsible for the site www.alcaralho.org5 and takes part in the Global Action of the Peoples and Local Action for Global Justice. When Noam Chomsky was in Brazil, he gave an interview to Pablo Ortellado.

There are around 200 volunteers all around Brazil who produce material in video, radio and articles and who manage the site (that is, it is their duty to “choose” or censor the material published at the site). These volunteers are divided into “collectives” and the largest ones are in São Paulo, Rio, and Rio Grande do Sul. The “collectives” are made of high school students, graduates, architects, physicists, journalists, system analysts among others. CMI does not keep an access register, assuring anonymity to anyone who makes threats or publishes lies.

According to Pablo Ortellado, in a declaration to the Union of Journalists of Paraná: “Since April 21st of last year, when the FBI filed a lawsuit asking for logs which would allow them to identify the 1.5 million hits and would end the right to privacy of our users, we have decided not to register any more hits. The story of why the FBI asked for the logs is long, but basically it was motivated by an article which made public the security plans during the protest in Quebec against the Americas Summit which was to discuss FTAA. If we handed in the logs, and we had a legal battle to prevent that, the American government would know exactly who accessed each of our materials and what time. It would be a huge attack against the freedom of the users. For that reason we do not register the logs. But, during the protests in Geneva in July last year, we had a problem with the software and we registered the logs for some hours. Making a projection with old data and what we knew in July, CMI Brazil had about 3 thousand daily hits, about a thousand users and during crises this access increases three or four times. In the global net, there are about 600 thousand hits and 100 thousand users, a considerable part of which is centered in the global site, which is managed by an international collective. But, in times of crisis, the number can go up to a million users.” http://www.sindijorpr.org.br/extrapauta/edicao57/pag25.htm (in Portuguese)

At the time of the terrorist attacks, Pablo Ortellado and his colleague, Moésio Rebouças, were the organizers of the first anti-American protest – just 15 days after more than 3 thousand Americans were murdered in the Twin Towers. They and others took advantage of the mobilization for a protest that had been organized against an IMF meeting which had been canceled because of the attacks and changed the objective to “peace against American militarism” (right after the largest foreign attack against the US in history!). The text of the link was made by Ortellado himself and shows his vision of how the antiglobalization movement originated (which has now taken off its mask and shows its true colors: anti-Americanism and anti-Semitic): http://www.elsonrezende.hpg.ig.com.br/textos/antiglob.html (in Portuguese)
